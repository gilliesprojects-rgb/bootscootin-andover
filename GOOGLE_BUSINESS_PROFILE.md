# Google Business Profile — Setup Guide for Boot Scootin' Andover

Google Business Profile (GBP) is the free listing that makes you show up in **Google Maps** and the
**local "map pack"** when someone searches *"line dancing near me"* or *"line dancing Andover"*.
It's the single highest-impact thing you can do for a local class business. It can only be set up by
you (Google verifies the owner), so this is a step-by-step you follow yourself.

---

## The key decision: Service-Area Business (recommended)

You run classes at **two community halls you don't own** (Abbotts Ann School and The Landale Wilson
Hall). Google's rules say you should only list an address you actually control and staff during
opening hours — so **don't** create two storefront listings at the halls.

Instead, create **one** profile as a **Service-Area Business (SAB)**:

- No public street address shown (you hide it — Google still asks for one to verify you're local).
- You set a **service area** instead: *Andover*, plus nearby villages (Abbotts Ann, Enham Alamein,
  Andover, Test Valley).
- The class venues and times live in your **website + posts**, which the listing links to.

This is the correct, policy-compliant setup for a class that travels between hired venues.

## Step-by-step

1. **Sign in with the right Google account.** Use the account you want to own this long-term —
   ideally `bootscootinuk@gmail.com` so the login matches the brand. (If you set up GA4/Search Console
   under a different Google account, that's fine — they don't have to match.)

2. Go to **https://business.google.com** → **"Add your business"** / **"Manage now"**.

3. **Business name:** `Boot Scootin' Andover`. If Google suggests an existing match, only claim it if
   it's genuinely yours.

4. **Category:** choose **Dance school** (primary). You can add more later.

5. **"Do you want to add a location customers can visit?"** → choose **No**.
   This makes it a Service-Area Business.

6. **Service area:** add **Andover**, then **Abbotts Ann**, **Enham Alamein**, and **Test Valley**.
   (You can list up to ~20 areas; keep them genuinely local.)

7. **Contact details:** phone `07717 844660`, website `https://www.bootscootin.co.uk`.

8. **Finish & verify.** Google will offer a verification method — usually **video verification**
   (record a short clip showing you, any branded kit/boots, and the area) or **phone/postcard**.
   *You cannot appear on Maps until verified*, so do this promptly. Video is fastest if offered.

## After it's live — fill it out fully (ranking depends on this)

A complete profile ranks far better than a bare one. Do all of these:

- **Description (750 chars):** who you are, the two venues, "beginners welcome, no partner needed",
  Andover/Hampshire. Work the phrase *line dancing in Andover* in naturally.
- **Photos:** add 8–10+ real photos — the logo, boots, people dancing, the venues. Profiles with
  photos get materially more clicks. Refresh them every few weeks.
- **Services:** add "Beginner line dancing class", "Improver line dancing class", "Country/western
  dance".
- **Opening hours:** Mon 18:15–20:30, Thu 19:15–20:15.
- **Booking link:** add your Bookwhen URL as the **appointment/booking link** so "Book" shows on the
  listing.
- **Products/Offers (optional):** list class prices here.
- **Posts:** publish a short **Update** post most weeks (new term starting, social night, "spaces
  this Monday"). Active profiles rank better and look alive.
- **Q&A:** seed a few obvious questions yourself ("Do I need a partner?", "I've never danced before —
  is that OK?", "Where do you meet?") and answer them.

---

## Reviews — the biggest ongoing ranking lever

- After it's verified, Google gives you a **short review link** (in the dashboard → "Ask for
  reviews"). Save it.
- Ask happy dancers to leave a Google review at the end of a class — even a one-liner. Aim for a
  steady trickle, not a burst.
- **Reply to every review**, good or bad. It's a ranking signal and it looks great to newcomers.

## How this ties into the website changes (already done)

Your site now outputs **LocalBusiness** and recurring **Event** structured data with both venue
addresses and class times. Keep the **name, phone, and area consistent** between the website and the
GBP listing — Google cross-checks them (this is called NAP consistency: Name, Address, Phone). Matching
details across your site, GBP, and any directory listings (e.g. local Facebook groups, danceweb,
community noticeboards) reinforces trust and ranking.
